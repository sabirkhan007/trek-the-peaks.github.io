Har Ki Dun Trek (7 Days / 6 Nights)

Discover the magic of the Garhwal Himalayas with the **Har Ki Dun Trek** – a timeless journey through the legendary **“Valley of Gods.”** Perfect for nature lovers and adventure seekers, this 7-day trek offers a stunning blend of alpine forests, lush meadows, glacial streams, and ancient Himalayan villages steeped in folklore.

Starting from the quaint village of **Sankri**, the trail gradually ascends to an altitude of **11,700 ft**, unveiling jaw-dropping views of the **Swargarohini**, **Black Peak**, and **Bandarpoonch** mountain ranges. As you walk through pine-scented forests and cross wooden bridges over crystal-clear rivers, every step feels like a scene from a storybook.

The Har Ki Dun trail isn’t just a trek—it's a deep dive into Himalayan culture, wildlife, and mythology. Spot exotic birds, vibrant alpine flowers, and encounter warm-hearted locals living in harmony with nature. This trek is best enjoyed in the blooming summers of **May–June** or the golden hues of **September–October**.

With cozy guesthouses, comfortable tents, hearty vegetarian meals, and an experienced trekking crew guiding the way, this unforgettable adventure promises more than just scenic beauty—it offers a soulful retreat into the heart of the Himalayas.

Itinerary

  • Start early morning from Dehradun and drive to Sankri (6,455 ft), covering approximately 210 km over 8-10 hours.
  • The drive passes through beautiful locales like Mussoorie, Kempty Falls, Naugaon, Purola, and Mori, alongside the Tons River.
  • Arrive at Sankri, a quaint Himalayan village that serves as the base camp for the trek. Overnight stay in a guest house or camp.

  • Drive from Sankri to Taluka (around 12 km), then begin the 14 km trek to Seema via the village of Gangad.
  • Trail passes through dense forests, waterfalls, and wooden bridges. Watch for Himalayan langurs, barking deer, and beautiful birds.
  • Reach Seema campsite (8,200 ft) and camp overnight near the roaring Supin River.

  • Today’s trek covers 11 km to reach the majestic Har Ki Dun valley situated at 11,500 ft.
  • Experience awe-inspiring views of the Swargarohini peaks, mountain ridges, ancient temples, and traditional wooden architecture.
  • Arrive at the campsite in Har Ki Dun, nestled in a wide cradle-shaped valley surrounded by snow-capped peaks. Overnight in tents.

  • Spend the day exploring the mesmerizing valley. Options include a hike to Maninda Tal or Jaundhar Glacier viewpoint.
  • Get panoramic views of Swargarohini I, II, and III, Black Peak (Kalanag), and Bandarpoonch range.
  • Enjoy the tranquility, wildflowers, and possibly encounter shepherds with their livestock. Return to campsite by evening.

  • Begin your return journey and retrace the 11 km trail from Har Ki Dun to Seema.
  • The descent offers new perspectives of the surrounding valley, dense pine forests, and crossing over wooden bridges.
  • Reach Seema by afternoon and relax at the riverside camp.

  • Trek 14 km back from Seema to Taluka through the same scenic trail.
  • Take the waiting vehicle and drive 12 km back to Sankri. Enjoy your final evening in the mountains with a warm local dinner.
  • Stay overnight at guest house or homestay in Sankri.

  • Depart early in the morning and drive back to Dehradun, reaching by evening (approximately 10 hours).
  • Bid farewell to your fellow trekkers and the serene Himalayas, carrying unforgettable memories of the Har Ki Dun trek.
